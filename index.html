Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need to pay the tuition fee for the Semester?
A: Your tuition fee for Semester 1 of 2026/27 will remain the same, regardless of whether you study at the HKU campus or at HKU CDS (Shanghai).
Q: What is the amount of learning subsidy?
A: You will receive a travel subsidy of RMB 3,000 and a living allowance of RMB 12,000 upon completion of the semester in Shanghai.
Q: Will the academic calendar align with Chinese Mainland holidays or Hong Kong's schedule?
A: The academic calendar will follow that of HKU and align with HKSAR public holidays.
Q: Do I still select courses via the portal, or is there a separate place?
A: You can enrol in courses via the SIS portal, just like other courses offered in Hong Kong. Look for the "SH" subclass.
Q: Will the final exam be held at the end of the semester or at the end of each block, and will the paper be the same as Hong Kong?
A: Examinations will be held at the end of the semester, concurrently with the Hong Kong examination period. The School will adopt the same examination procedure for courses offered in both Hong Kong and Shanghai.
Q: Do I need to pay the dormitory fee?
A: No. A free student dormitory (double occupancy in a 2-bedroom apartment) will be provided during your studies in Shanghai.
Q: When can I check in to the student dormitory?
A: Dormitory check-in will commence on 29 August 2026.
Q: How far is the student dormitory from the teaching site, and is there a shuttle bus?
A: The student dormitory is approximately a 20-minute walk from the teaching site, and a shuttle bus will be provided.
Q: Can I choose my roommate?
A: Room allocations, including apartment pairings, are managed randomly by the Campus Service Office to foster integration.
Q: What if I can't open a mainland bank account before 30 June?
A: If you are unable to open one in advance, you can use the on-campus bank counter services during Orientation Day (31 August).
Q: Is attendance mandatory for the Reading Week activities?
A: No, corporate visits and career development talks during the Reading Week are completely optional. You are free to plan your personal travel or study schedule instead.
